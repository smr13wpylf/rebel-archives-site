There's a particular sentence that should make you pause: any sentence where spiritual vocabulary is doing the work that, in a more honest conversation, would be done by a demand.

*"I'm just holding space for your growth."* *"That's your shadow talking."* *"I have to honor my energy."* Each of these can be true and kind. Each can also be a power move in a robe.

## The mechanism

Spiritual language has a specific advantage in conflict: it's unfalsifiable and it claims the high ground at the same time. If I tell you that your objection is "just your ego," I've done something clever. I've reframed your disagreement as a symptom, made my position the healthy one, and exempted myself from having to actually answer you.

> The fastest way to win an argument is to redefine your opponent's reasoning as a wound. Then you're not fighting them — you're helping them.

This is bypassing, but pointed outward. We usually talk about spiritual bypassing as something people do to avoid their own pain. Less discussed is how the same vocabulary gets used to manage other people — to pathologize their resistance, launder a preference into a boundary, or dress control as care.

## How to spot it

A few signals that language has stopped describing and started managing:

- **The framework only ever flows one direction.** Your behavior is "projection"; theirs is "discernment." Your anger is "unhealed"; theirs is "righteous."
- **Disagreement is treated as a developmental stage.** If you don't accept the premise, you're simply "not ready" — and there's no version of you, short of agreement, that counts as ready.
- **The vocabulary forecloses the conversation rather than opening it.** Real spiritual language tends to make things more spacious. Weaponized spiritual language makes them airless.

## Why this matters for the work

None of this means the concepts are worthless. Shadow is real. Energy is a useful word. Boundaries are necessary. The problem isn't the vocabulary — it's the moment the vocabulary stops being a way of seeing more clearly and becomes a way of not having to.

Discernment, here, is everything. You can hold the depth of these ideas and still notice when someone — including yourself — is using them to avoid a plain human conversation. Usually the avoided conversation is about power: who has it, who wants it, and who's being asked to call its exercise something holier than it is.
